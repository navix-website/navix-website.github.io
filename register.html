Hello, future Navixian!


This page will allow you to start the registration process to board one of Navix's tours.
Registration has three steps, and it's relatively simple process.
After registration, you will recieve an email from Navix containing further information.
So take a deep breath, and let's get started!


Please note the following as you start your Navix registration process:

  • If you are registering for multiple people at the same time, you will need complete the registration process separately for each registrant. However, you can pay for your group as a whole at once. Payment can be made after registration.
  • All of Navix's tours are launched from sites in the United States, and we operate under United States jurisdiction. As such, if you wish to undertake Navix's tours from another country, you will need to make arrangements for a legal visit to the United States.
  • Do not leave this page after starting registration. Your progress will be lost.

Now that you've finished registering on the site, go through the following instructions to know what to do next:

  • Step 1: Check your email
    Check your primary email for an email from Navix, and make sure all your details are accurate. If you think the details you entered are inaccurate, register again.
    If you did not get the email:
    • If you entered a secondary email, check your secondary email.
    • If you did not enter a secondary email, register again and check your email properly.

  • Step 2: Make the payment
    The amount due is:
    Make the required payment using the details given in the email.
    You will have upto 3 days after registration to make your payment.

  • Step 3: Keep an eye out further correspondence and ready required documents
    After your payment has been verified and processed (usually 2-3 business days), you will recieve further information regarding prerequisite training and your health check-up.
    You will also be asked for the following documents after payment to be uploaded online:
    • A proof of identity (for example, your passport or driving license)
    • If you live outside the United States, your passport, travel documentation and proof of your right of residence in the US
