Date of Launch Procedure

  • On the date of the scheduled launch, passengers will arrive via shuttle bus at the designated launch location. When they arrive, there will be Navix staff waiting to direct the patrons. You will then be led into a room where you will change into your specialized aerospace suits. From there, one of the flight controllers will brief the passengers on the whole tour. After all the safety and equipment checks, the passengers will be ready to go out onto the plane (or) rocket for the experience of a lifetime.

This is an image of one of our launches for the Pinnacle tour.

Flight

  1. When the passengers have boarded the nose of the rocket, they will take a commemorative picture with everyone on board to celebrate the momentous occasion (a picture with the Challenger plane if you're taking the Around the World tour). Everyone will then take their seats and strap in, bracing themselves for liftoff, which will be rough.

  2. At liftoff, you will suddenly feel a great force pushing down on you as you look out the window to see the ground leaving. It will take approximately ten minutes to escape the atmosphere and into orbit (20 minutes for the Challenger), at which point you will be traveling faster than you've ever known.

  3. After you're in space, you'll notice that you are weightless. Before long, you'll all get the go ahead to unstrap and move around the ship to enjoy the stay, with differing tour parameters for each tour. Full flight events will be listed in you debriefing document.

This is an image of a passenger exploring the ISS for the ISS tour.

Recovery

  1. When the time comes to say goodbye to the beautiful expanse of space and return home to earth, everyone will need to take their seats and prepare for reentry. Automatic boosters on the outside of the rocket will decelerate the ship and it will start to fly or fall back to Earth. Upon reentry into Earth's atmosphere, you will gradually decelerate.

  2. While the ride home may not feel the smoothest, we've made sure that the engineering of the capsule or plane will ensure a safe landing. On the outer surface of all vehicles, there is a heat shield designed to resist the heat caused by air resistance while in reentry, and we've implemented auto-correcting systems onboard.

  3. For the Pinnacle and ISS tours, at a certain altitude, a parachute will be deployed from the capsule to slow down the descent, and the capsule will right itself vertically. After approximately thirty minutes, you will be around 800 feet from the predetermined landing location. At this point, the thrusters on the bottom of the capsule will activate in a series of bursts so that the capsule will land vertically.
